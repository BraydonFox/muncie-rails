The Wysor Street Depot

The depot was built in 1901 by the Cincinnati, Richmond, and Muncie Railroad. The CR&M would later be acquired by the Chesapeake and Ohio Railway in 1910. The C&O ended passenger service to Muncie in 1949, and the depot was then used for freight services until the Muncie Gear Works began leasing the building.

1973 saw the incorporation of the "Chessie System", a holding company composed of the Chesapeake and Ohio and other railroad companies. The Chessie System restored the depot to service passengers on Amtrak's "Cardinal". The depot itself was not open to passengers, and only the outisde platform was used.

Amtrak's Cardinal meets the Chessie Steam Special near Muncie in 1978. Photo by Brian Woodruff; rrpicturearchives.net

In 1985, Amtrak re-routed The Cardinal, and Muncie lost its last passenger train. With no passengers to serve, the depot was left abandonded by Chessie Systems successor, CSX Transportation.

In 1993, Cardinal Greenways, Inc. purchased the Wysor Street Depot, along with 60 miles of former railroad right-of-way.

Today, the station is beautifully restored to its former glory and serves as a visitor's center and trailhead for the Cardinal Greenway. The Cardinal Greenway uses the roadbed that used to support the tracks of the C&O railroad.

Although the C&O tracks are long gone, another parallel rail line (part of Norfolk Southern's New Castle District) is still in operation, separated from the trail by a safety fence.
